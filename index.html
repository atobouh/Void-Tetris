The "Native Speaker Trap"

Fluent speakers fail because they treat the test like a casual conversation.

  • DO NOT be creative. Be boring. Be predictable.
  • DO NOT say "Umm" or "Uh." Silence is better than fillers.
  • DO NOT give your opinion unless specifically asked (Task 1 only).
  • Spelling: Pick ONE style (US or UK) and stick to it. Don't mix Colour and Center.

The "Cheat Code" Templates

Memorize these exact words. They force you to be structured.

Speaking Task 1 (The Personal Choice)

"Do you prefer X or Y?"

"Personally, I prefer [Option X] for two main reasons.

First, it is much more efficient/enjoyable. For example, [Personal Story about how X helped you].

Second, it is safer/better/cheaper. For instance, [Specific Detail].

That is why I think [Option X] is better."

Speaking Task 2-4 (Integrated)

Read/Listen -> Summarize. No opinions.

"The reading passage proposes a change to [Topic]. The speaker in the audio opposes this plan for two main reasons.

First, the speaker argues that [Reason 1]. He mentions that [Detail from audio].

Second, he claims that [Reason 2]. For example, he notes that [Detail].

Therefore, the speaker strongly disagrees with the proposal."

Writing: Academic Discussion

10 Minutes. 100+ Words.

"This is a relevant topic. While I understand [Student A]'s point about [Point A], I agree more with [Student B] that [Point B].

I would add that [Your New Idea]. For example, in my country, [Personal Example].

Therefore, I believe [Summary of your view]."
